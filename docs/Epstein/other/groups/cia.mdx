## Overview

The CIA's alleged connection to Jeffrey Epstein is less direct than the [Mossad](mossad.md) connection but is supported by several notable data points. The most significant is the allegation that federal prosecutor Alexander Acosta — who gave Epstein the controversial 2008 plea deal — told the Trump transition team that he was instructed to give Epstein a lenient deal because Epstein "belonged to intelligence" and he should "leave it alone."

**Important caveat:** The CIA denies any connection to Epstein. The evidence is largely circumstantial. What follows documents the allegations, not proven facts.

## The Acosta Statement

The most direct evidence of intelligence involvement comes from Alexander Acosta's alleged statement during his vetting for Secretary of Labor. According to reporting by Vicky Ward in *The Daily Beast* (2019), when asked about the lenient Epstein plea deal, Acosta reportedly said:

> "I was told Epstein 'belonged to intelligence' and to leave it alone."

Acosta has not publicly confirmed or denied this specific quote.

## The Southern Air Transport Connection

**Southern Air Transport** was an airline founded in 1947 and **owned outright by the CIA from 1960 to 1973**. It was used in Iran-Contra arms smuggling. After being ostensibly privatized, Epstein reportedly helped Les Wexner repurpose the former CIA planes to ship lingerie for Wexner's L Brands empire. In 1996, Southern Air Transport relocated its headquarters to **Columbus, Ohio** — Wexner's base — to deliver products from Hong Kong/China factories. Reported by Drop Site News in their investigation "Epstein, Israel, and the CIA: How the Iran-Contra Planes Landed at Les Wexner's Base."

## The Donald Barr Connection

Donald Barr was headmaster of the Dalton School, an elite Manhattan prep school, when he hired 20-year-old Jeffrey Epstein as a math and physics teacher in 1974. This was remarkable because Epstein had dropped out of college. Barr had previously worked for the Office of Strategic Services (OSS), the CIA's predecessor agency. His son William Barr later became the Attorney General overseeing the Epstein case.



# Max Spiers

British conspiracy researcher investigating elite pedophilia networks who died in Poland at 39 after telling his mother "if anything happens to me, investigate."

## Key Facts

- **Full Name:** Maxwell Bates-Spiers (Max Spiers)
- **Born:** January 22, 1986, Canterbury, England
- **Died:** July 16, 2016, Warsaw, Poland (age 39)
- **Cause of Death:** Pneumonia combined with drug intoxication (alprazolam and oxycodone)
- **Official Ruling:** Drug-related death (2019 inquest)

## Connection to Epstein

Max Spiers' connection to the Epstein case is through his research into elite pedophilia networks:

- Spiers was investigating what he believed was an elite pedophilia ring involving powerful individuals — the same type of network later documented in the Epstein case
- His mother stated he was researching "an organisation he believed was involved in running a paedophile ring"
- His death occurred in July 2016 — three years before Epstein's arrest but during the same period other trafficking researchers were dying ([Monica Petersen](Monica_Petersen.md) died four months later)
- The pattern of conspiracy researchers and journalists investigating elite pedophilia dying (Spiers 2016, [Isaac Kappy](Isaac_Kappy.md) 2019, [Tracy Twyman](Tracy_Twyman.md) 2019) is part of the broader Epstein-adjacent death pattern

## Circumstances of Death

On July 16, 2016, in Warsaw, Poland:

- Spiers was visiting a friend when he was found dead on a sofa
- Polish authorities initially ruled the death as "natural causes" without conducting a thorough post-mortem
- A 2019 inquest in the UK found he died of pneumonia combined with drug intoxication — specifically alprazolam (Xanax equivalent) at twice the therapeutic dose and potentially fatal levels of oxycodone
- The UK coroner criticized the Polish police investigation as "wholly incompetent" for failing to conduct a proper post-mortem
- Spiers had sustained a cracked pelvis in the US and developed an opioid use disorder from prescribed painkillers

## Warning to His Mother

Two days before his death, Spiers told his mother Vanessa Bates:
- "Your boy's in trouble. If anything happens to me, investigate."
- His mother told The Daily Telegraph she believed Max "had been digging in dark places and feared somebody wanted him dead"

## Assessment

The 2019 inquest determined drug-related death, and Spiers had a documented opioid use disorder. However, the "wholly incompetent" initial Polish investigation, his warning to his mother, and his research into elite pedophilia networks place this case alongside other researchers who died while investigating similar topics.

**Suspicion Level:** UNCERTAIN — Drug intoxication with documented opioid disorder, but warned his mother to investigate and was researching elite pedophilia.

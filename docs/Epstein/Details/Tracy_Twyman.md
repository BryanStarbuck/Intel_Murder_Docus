## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

Twyman died by hanging two months after fellow researcher Isaac Kappy — whose Epstein-related research she was actively continuing — and approximately one month before Epstein's arrest. Most significantly, just 11 days after Kappy's death she recorded a detailed "dead man's switch" video documenting death threats against her, a hacked computer, and specific intelligence about an Arizona campground connected to her research. The combination of a recorded warning, documented threats, and the precise timing between Kappy's death and Epstein's arrest makes her case among the more notable in the cluster of suspicious deaths surrounding Epstein-adjacent researchers.

## Background
Tracy Twyman was a podcast producer, author of approximately seven books on occult and esoteric history, and independent researcher. In 2018, she began investigating [Isaac Kappy](Isaac_Kappy.md)'s claims about pedophilia in Hollywood and connections to [Jeffrey Epstein](Jeffrey_Epstein.md)'s trafficking network. She was undeterred by Kappy's death in May 2019 and continued the research, focusing on networks she believed connected Hollywood, elite pedophilia rings, and the Epstein operation.

## Circumstances of Death

Twyman was found hanged in her garage in July 2019. The exact date and location were not publicly disclosed at her family's request. Her husband was investigated by law enforcement and formally ruled out as a suspect. The official ruling was suicide by hanging.

The circumstances surrounding her death became significant to researchers when the existence of her pre-recorded "dead man's switch" video became public. On May 24, 2019 — just 11 days after Isaac Kappy's death — Twyman had recorded a 27-minute video and entrusted it to two friends, to be released if she died. The video detailed death threats she had received, described her computer being hacked, and drew connections between her situation and Kappy's death. She also mentioned a campground in Arizona that she had investigated and which she said was suddenly closed after her inquiry.

## The Counterargument

The official ruling is suicide. Her husband was investigated and formally cleared. The Pasco County death was handled by local law enforcement, not suppressed or rushed. Twyman had been researching deeply conspiratorial subject matter and had been active in online communities where paranoia about surveillance and persecution was commonplace — the threats she documented could reflect genuine harassment from bad actors in that community rather than government operatives. No physical evidence of foul play was reported. The "dead man's switch" video, while striking, is evidence that she feared being killed, not that she was actually killed by the parties she named.

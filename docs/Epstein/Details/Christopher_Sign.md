## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

Christopher Sign gained national prominence for breaking one of the most politically damaging stories of the 2016 election cycle — the secret meeting between Bill Clinton and Attorney General Loretta Lynch on a Phoenix airport tarmac while Hillary Clinton was under FBI investigation. Sign reported receiving significant death threats against himself and his family after the story broke. He wrote a book, *Secret on the Tarmac*, detailing the story and its aftermath. He was found dead at his home at age 45, survived by his wife and three young children. The death was ruled a suicide, but the circumstances — given his documented threats and the political sensitivity of his reporting — have drawn scrutiny.

## Circumstances of Death

On Saturday, June 12, 2021, at approximately 8:00 a.m., Christopher Sign was found dead at his home in Hoover, Alabama. Local authorities investigated and classified the death as an apparent suicide. Sign's employer, ABC 33/40 in Birmingham, confirmed his passing. He was survived by his wife Laura and their three sons.

No detailed public account of the specific circumstances has been released by authorities. Sign was 45 years old and, by all public accounts, actively engaged in his career and family life at the time of his death.

## Background

Christopher Paul Sign grew up in Arlington, Texas, where he was an all-state offensive lineman at Lamar High School. He played football at the University of Alabama under Coach Gene Stallings in the 1990s, signing as a left guard for the Crimson Tide in 1994. He graduated with a journalism degree and began his television career.

Sign worked as a reporter and anchor at multiple ABC affiliates, including ABC 33/40 in Birmingham (2000-2005) and ABC15 in Phoenix, Arizona. It was during his time at ABC15 in Phoenix that he broke the story that would define his career.

On June 27, 2016, Sign reported that former President Bill Clinton had boarded Attorney General Loretta Lynch's plane on the tarmac at Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport for a private meeting. The meeting occurred while Hillary Clinton was under active FBI investigation over her use of a private email server as Secretary of State. Both Clinton and Lynch claimed they discussed only personal topics such as grandchildren and golf. The meeting became a major political scandal, and Lynch later said she regretted the encounter. FBI Director James Comey later testified that the tarmac meeting was a factor in his decision to make a public statement about the Clinton email investigation.

In 2019, Sign published *Secret on the Tarmac*, a book detailing his experience breaking the story and its aftermath. He returned to ABC 33/40 in Birmingham in 2017, where he worked as a morning anchor until his death.

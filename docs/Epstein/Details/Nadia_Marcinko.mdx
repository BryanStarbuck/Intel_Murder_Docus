## Assessment: MISSING PERSON AT RISK

Nadia Marcinko (formerly known as Nada Marcinkova) was reportedly trafficked by Epstein from Slovakia to the United States as a teenager. Epstein allegedly told associates he had "purchased" her from her parents. She later became one of Epstein's pilots, flying his Gulfstream aircraft after receiving her pilot's license in 2012, and served as a long-term assistant. She was one of four people granted immunity in Epstein's controversial 2008 non-prosecution agreement. She has not been seen at her known addresses since early January 2024, shortly after the unsealing of the final batch of Epstein court documents.

## Background

Marcinko was born in Slovakia and was allegedly brought to the United States by Epstein as a teenager. [Virginia Giuffre](Virginia_Giuffre.md) and other accusers identified her as both a victim and a participant in Epstein's abuse network. She was named in multiple court filings and depositions.

After Epstein's 2008 plea deal, she reinvented herself as an aviation entrepreneur, founding Aviloop, an aviation website, and working as a certified flight instructor. She became a licensed pilot in 2012 and flew Epstein's aircraft as part of his pilot team, alongside chief pilot Larry Visoski and David Rodgers.

She was granted immunity from federal prosecution as part of the 2008 non-prosecution agreement negotiated by Alexander Acosta. No criminal charges were ever filed against her.

## Disappearance

In early January 2024, shortly after the unsealing of the final batch of Epstein court documents, Marcinko disappeared from her Upper East Side apartment in New York City, located near Epstein's former mansion. The Independent reported her missing in January 2024.

As of late 2025, OSINT investigators reported that her email account appeared to still be active, suggesting she may be alive but in hiding. However, she has not been seen publicly or at her known addresses.

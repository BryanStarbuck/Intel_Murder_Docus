

# Gary Caradori

Private investigator for the Nebraska Franklin scandal who died with his 8-year-old son when his plane disintegrated mid-air; briefcase of evidence went missing.

## Key Facts

- **Full Name:** Gary Caradori
- **Born:** Unknown
- **Died:** July 11, 1990, near Ashton, Illinois
- **Cause of Death:** Plane crash — aircraft disintegrated mid-air
- **Official Ruling:** Pilot error (NTSB)
- **Also Killed:** His 8-year-old son, Andrew "AJ" Caradori

## Circumstances of Death

On July 11, 1990, between 2:40 a.m. and 2:57 a.m.:

- Caradori was flying his single-engine Piper Saratoga from Chicago back to Nebraska
- He had been on a trip to collect new evidence — reportedly photographs said to implicate Lawrence King in child abuse
- The plane disintegrated mid-air approximately four miles south of Ashton, Illinois, about 100 miles west of Chicago
- Wreckage was scattered over a wide area consistent with in-flight breakup
- His briefcase, allegedly containing the newly obtained photographic evidence, was never recovered from the crash site
- The NTSB ruled the crash was caused by pilot error
- Caradori's brother and Nebraska State Senator Loran Schmit both suspected foul play

## The Franklin Scandal Context

- In 1988, the Franklin Community Federal Credit Union in Omaha was raided, revealing a $40 million embezzlement scheme by Lawrence King
- Multiple young adults came forward claiming that as minors they had been trafficked to parties where they were abused by prominent men
- The Nebraska Legislature formed a special committee and hired Caradori to investigate
- Caradori conducted extensive interviews and gathered over 21 hours of videotaped testimony
- After Caradori's death, a state grand jury dismissed the allegations as a "carefully crafted hoax" and indicted two of the accusers for perjury
- The FBI was later criticized for failing to investigate the allegations properly

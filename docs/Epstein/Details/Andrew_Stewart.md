## Assessment: SUSPICIOUS

Andrew "Chef Andy" Stewart was identified by Maria Farmer — one of the earliest and most credible Epstein accusers — as Epstein and Maxwell's longtime personal chef who "traveled everywhere with them for years." Farmer called him the "holy grail" of her case because "he knew everything." According to investigative journalist Whitney Webb, Stewart died suddenly in his sleep in April 2020 just as Farmer's lawyers were seeking to subpoena him for information on the Epstein case. His death occurred eight months after Epstein's death and three months before Ghislaine Maxwell's arrest. No specific cause of death was publicly disclosed. His name appears in the Epstein flight logs. No major news outlet covered the Epstein connection to his death.

## Circumstances of Death

According to the Grand Haven Tribune and his obituary at Betzler Life Story Funeral Homes, Andrew Stewart "died peacefully in his sleep" on approximately April 7, 2020, in Kalamazoo, Michigan. No specific medical cause of death was publicly disclosed. His obituary makes no mention of any connection to Jeffrey Epstein.

According to investigative journalist Whitney Webb, who broke the connection publicly on April 24, 2020, Stewart died "just as Farmer's lawyers were seeking to subpoena him for information on the Epstein case." Maria Farmer and her lawyers were reportedly unaware of Stewart's death until after Farmer's recorded phone call with Webb was published.

## Background

Stewart had a 30+ year career in the restaurant industry in Michigan and New York:

- Grew up in Kalamazoo, Michigan, attending Winchell Elementary, Maple Street Middle School, and Loy Norrix High School
- Graduated from the College of Wooster, Ohio
- Worked at several Kalamazoo-area restaurants including J Ross Browne's Whaling Station and Chi Chi's
- Co-owned Charron's, a fine dining restaurant in Kalamazoo
- Became **lead buyer of fresh fish at the Fulton Street Fish Market in New York City** — this is the likely period when he connected with the Epstein/Maxwell household
- Owned The Fishmonger, processing fish for area restaurants
- Most recently served as **corporate chef at Fustini's Oils & Vinegars** in downtown Holland, Michigan, where he taught cooking classes

His family included his wife Nancy Milligan (married 1981), children Samantha and Teddy, and brother John Stewart.

## The Counterargument

- Stewart was 62 years old; sudden death during sleep is not uncommon at that age, particularly from cardiac events
- No autopsy results have been publicly released, and there is no forensic evidence of foul play
- The identification of Stewart as Epstein's chef rests primarily on Maria Farmer's account, though this is supported by his presence in the flight logs
- The COVID-19 pandemic was underway, complicating any investigation and making sudden deaths less notable in the public eye
- His obituary and local news coverage make no mention of any Epstein connection, which could indicate the connection was less significant than alleged
- Many people appear in the Epstein flight logs without being deeply involved in his criminal operations

## Assessment: HIGHLY SUSPICIOUS

Kuzminov defected to Ukraine by flying a Russian Mi-8 military helicopter across enemy lines in August 2023. Six months later, he was found dead in a Spanish parking garage, his body "riddled with bullet holes and run over by a car." The ammunition used was Russian-made. Russian intelligence chief Sergei Naryshkin had publicly called him a "traitor" and "moral corpse" — language widely interpreted as a death sentence. Spanish police identified two persons of interest with Russian intelligence backgrounds but closed the investigation unable to identify the killers.

## Circumstances of Death

On February 13, 2024, Kuzminov's bullet-riddled body was found in an underground parking garage in Villajoyosa, a resort town on Spain's Mediterranean coast. He had been shot multiple times and his body had been run over by a car. The extreme violence — shooting and vehicular mutilation — was consistent with a punitive killing intended to send a message.

The ammunition recovered at the scene was Russian-manufactured, "leading back to Moscow." Spanish authorities identified two "persons of interest" who were in Spain at the time of the murder: one was described as a former KGB officer and the other, his relative, was identified as a Russian police colonel.

Despite these leads, Spanish police closed the investigation in late 2025, stating they were unable to identify the perpetrators or the organizer of the murder.

## Background

Maxim Kuzminov was a Russian military helicopter pilot who defected to Ukraine in an operation coordinated by Ukraine's Main Directorate of Intelligence (GUR), codenamed "Operation Synytsia." The operation took over six months to plan and culminated on August 9, 2023, when Kuzminov turned off radio communications during a flight and landed his Mi-8 helicopter near Poltava in Ukraine's Kharkiv Oblast. Ukrainian forces received him; the other two Russian crew members were reportedly killed during the operation.

The defection was a significant intelligence and propaganda victory for Ukraine, and Kuzminov reportedly received Ukrainian citizenship and financial compensation. He subsequently relocated to Spain, where he was living under a new identity at the time of his death.

After his defection, Russian intelligence officials made threatening public statements. SVR Director Sergei Naryshkin called Kuzminov a "moral corpse," adding: "A dog's death awaits him." Russian media extensively demonized him as a traitor.
